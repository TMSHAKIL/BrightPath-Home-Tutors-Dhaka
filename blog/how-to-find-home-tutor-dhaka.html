1. Why Choosing the Right Tutor Matters

In Dhaka, competition among SSC and HSC students is intense. With thousands of students competing for golden A+ grades in board exams and university admission tests, having the right home tutor is no longer a luxury — it is a necessity for many families.

But not every tutor is the right fit. A tutor who is excellent at HSC Physics may not be the right person for a Class 7 student struggling with basic Mathematics. Matching the right tutor to the right student is the most important step in the entire process.

💡 Key Insight

Studies show that students who receive personalized one-on-one tutoring perform on average 2 grades higher than those who rely only on classroom teaching. The key word is personalized — a good tutor adapts to the student, not the other way around.

2. What to Look for in a Home Tutor in Dhaka

When searching for a home tutor in Dhaka, most parents focus only on the tutor's university or degree. While academic qualification matters, it is only one of several important factors.

Academic Qualification

For SSC students, a tutor who is studying in or has graduated from a good college or university is usually sufficient. For HSC Physics, Chemistry, or Mathematics, look for tutors from BUET, Dhaka University, or medical colleges. For O-Level and A-Level students, the tutor should have direct experience with Cambridge or Edexcel curricula.

Teaching Experience

A brilliant student does not automatically make a brilliant tutor. Look for someone with at least 1–2 years of tutoring experience and ask for references from previous students or parents in Dhaka.

Subject Specialization

Avoid tutors who claim to teach every subject. A true specialist in HSC Higher Mathematics will produce better results than a generalist who teaches 8 different subjects. Always match the tutor to the specific subject your child needs.

✅ Tutor Evaluation Checklist
  • ✓ Degree from a recognized university in relevant subject
  • ✓ Minimum 1–2 years tutoring experience in Dhaka
  • ✓ NID card verified and academic certificates checked
  • ✓ References from at least 2 previous students or parents
  • ✓ Lives near your area in Dhaka for punctuality
  • ✓ Willing to do a free trial class before committing
  • ✓ Communicates progress regularly with parents

3. Where to Find Home Tutors in Dhaka

There are several ways to find home tutors in Dhaka. Each has its advantages and risks.

Word of Mouth

Asking neighbors, relatives, or other parents in your area is still one of the most common ways to find a tutor in Dhaka. The advantage is trust — someone you know has already verified the tutor. The disadvantage is limited options and no formal verification process.

Facebook Groups

Many Dhaka parents search Facebook groups like "Tutor Wanted Dhaka" or area-specific groups. This gives you a large number of options but comes with zero verification. You have no way to confirm the tutor's credentials without doing your own background check.

Tutor Matching Services

Services like BrightPath screen tutors before listing them — checking their NID, academic certificates, and conducting a trial evaluation. This saves parents time and provides significantly more safety, especially for families with younger children.

💡 BrightPath Tip

Always insist on a free trial class before making any payment. Any genuine tutor or tutor service in Dhaka should be willing to offer this. If they refuse, it is a red flag.

4. Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Home Tutor in Dhaka

Before you confirm a tutor for your child, ask these questions directly:

  • What is your educational background and which university did you graduate from?
  • How many students are you currently teaching in Dhaka? — Too many students means less attention for your child.
  • Can you share references from two previous students or their parents?
  • How do you track and report a student's progress?
  • What is your approach if the student is not improving?
  • Are you available for the schedule we need?
  • Can we start with a free trial class?

5. Red Flags to Avoid When Hiring a Tutor in Dhaka

Unfortunately, not every tutor advertising in Dhaka is qualified or trustworthy. Watch out for these warning signs:

  • Refuses to provide academic certificates — A qualified tutor should have no problem showing their degree or marksheet.
  • Claims to teach all subjects and all levels — Genuine specialists focus on specific subjects.
  • Asks for advance payment before a trial class — Always insist on a free trial first.
  • Cannot provide references — Every experienced tutor in Dhaka should have at least a few satisfied parents who can vouch for them.
  • Irregular attendance history — Ask about their punctuality record with previous students.

However, tutor quality does not always correlate with location — a BUET-graduate tutor in Mirpur may be significantly better than an average tutor in Gulshan charging double the rate.

💡 Money Saving Tip

Do not choose a tutor based on price alone. A slightly more expensive but highly qualified tutor will produce better results faster — saving you money in the long run by reducing the number of months of tutoring needed.
